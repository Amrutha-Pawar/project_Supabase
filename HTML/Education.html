EDUCATION

The Indian education system is a complex and multifaceted system that has undergone significant changes over the years.
Here are some key aspects of the Indian education system: Structure of Education in India 1. Primary Education (Class 1-5): This stage focuses on basic literacy and numeracy skills.
2. Upper Primary Education (Class 6-8): Students learn various subjects, including languages, mathematics, science, and social studies.
3. Secondary Education (Class 9-10): Students take a board exam at the end of Class 10, which determines their future academic path.
4. Higher Secondary Education (Class 11-12): Students choose a stream (science, commerce, or humanities) and prepare for university entrance exams.
5. Undergraduate and Postgraduate Education: Students pursue degrees in various fields, such as engineering, medicine, arts, and commerce.

Types of Educational Institutions 1. Government Schools: Run by the government, these schools provide free or subsidized education.
2. Private Schools: Run by private organizations, these schools charge fees and often offer better infrastructure and facilities.
3. International Schools: Offer international curricula, such as the International Baccalaureate (IB) or Cambridge International Examinations (CIE).
4. Universities and Colleges: Offer undergraduate and postgraduate programs in various fields.
